🏟️ COLOSSEUM

The Iconic Amphitheatre of Ancient Rome

🏛️ About the Colosseum

The Colosseum is one of the greatest monuments of Ancient Rome.
It is located in Rome, Italy, and is the largest amphitheatre ever built by the Romans.
It was used for gladiator fights, public events, and entertainment.
Today it is one of the most famous tourist attractions in the world.

📜 History

Construction began in 72 AD under Emperor Vespasian.
It was completed in 80 AD by Emperor Titus.
The Colosseum hosted gladiator contests, animal hunts, and public shows.

⭐ Why Famous

• Largest Roman amphitheatre.
• Home of gladiator battles.
• UNESCO World Heritage Site.
• One of the New Seven Wonders.

🏛️ Architecture

Built from stone and concrete.
Four levels with large arches.
Oval shape for 50,000 spectators.
Amazing Roman engineering.

🎯 Purpose

Used for gladiator fights, animal shows, public ceremonies, and entertainment.

✨ Interesting Facts

• Nearly 2,000 years old.
• Held 50,000 spectators.
• Underground tunnels below arena.
• Famous worldwide.

🌍 Europe
🏛️ Built: 72–80 AD
