🗽 Statue of Liberty

A Symbol of Freedom and Hope


ℹ️ About

The Statue of Liberty is a world-famous monument located on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, United States. Officially named Liberty Enlightening the World, it was a gift from France to the United States to celebrate friendship and the ideals of liberty and democracy. It was designed by Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi and its framework was engineered by Gustave Eiffel. It was dedicated on October 28, 1886. The statue represents freedom, hope, and democracy.

📜 History

The history of the Statue of Liberty began in 1865 when Édouard de Laboulaye proposed a monument celebrating the friendship between France and America. Construction started in France in 1875. The statue was shipped to New York in 350 pieces and rebuilt on Liberty Island. It was officially dedicated in 1886.

⭐ Why Famous

The Statue of Liberty is famous because it is a global symbol of freedom, democracy, and human rights. It welcomed millions of immigrants arriving in America and is one of the most visited landmarks in the world.

🎯 Purpose

The Statue of Liberty was built to celebrate friendship between France and the United States. It represents freedom, peace, democracy, and hope.

🏛️ Architecture

The Statue of Liberty is designed in the Neoclassical style. It is made of copper sheets over an iron and steel framework designed by Gustave Eiffel. It is about 93 meters tall and has a crown with seven rays.

✨ Interesting Facts

  • Official name: Liberty Enlightening the World.
  • Dedicated on October 28, 1886.
  • The crown has seven rays representing continents and seas.
  • The torch symbolizes enlightenment and freedom.
  • Copper changes color into green patina over time.
  • Millions of visitors visit every year.
