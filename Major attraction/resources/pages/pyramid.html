Discover Giza

🏜 About

The Pyramids of Giza are one of the most famous historical monuments in the world. Located on the Giza Plateau near Cairo, Egypt, they are the last surviving wonder of the Ancient Seven Wonders of the World. Built over 4,500 years ago during the Fourth Dynasty for the pharaohs Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure, the pyramids served as royal tombs. The Great Pyramid of Khufu is the largest and was originally 146.6 meters tall. Today, the Pyramids of Giza are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of ancient Egyptian civilization, attracting millions of visitors every year.

📜 History

The history of the Pyramids of Giza began around 2580–2560 BC during the Fourth Dynasty of ancient Egypt. They were built as royal tombs for the pharaohs Khufu, Khafre, and Menkaure. The Great Pyramid of Khufu is the largest and most famous of the three. Thousands of skilled workers used millions of limestone and granite blocks to build these magnificent structures without modern machines. Today, the Pyramids of Giza are a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the world's most famous historical landmarks, attracting millions of visitors every year.

⭐ Why Famous

The Pyramids of Giza are famous because they are the last surviving Wonder of the Ancient World. Millions of people visit them every year.

💡 Interesting Facts

The Great Pyramid was once the tallest building in the world. It was built using about 2.3 million stone blocks.

🎯 Purpose

The main purpose of the Pyramids of Giza was to serve as royal tombs for the pharaohs of ancient Egypt. The Egyptians believed in life after death, so the pyramids were built to protect the pharaohs' bodies and their treasures. They also symbolized the power, wealth, and religious beliefs of the Egyptian rulers.

🏛 Architecture

The pyramids have a square base and four triangular sides. They were built using limestone and granite blocks with amazing accuracy. The Great Pyramid was originally about 146.6 meters high.
